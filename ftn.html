01The Challenge

Spectrum is finite. Demand is not.

Mobile, satellite, and defense networks share a single fundamental constraint: bandwidth. As traffic grows exponentially, regulators auction scarce spectrum at record prices, and operators race to extract every bit of capacity from each Hertz they own. For decades, the Nyquist rate set the ceiling on what was possible. That ceiling was never absolute.

Faster-than-Nyquist signaling rewrites the rules — without exceeding the spectrum mask, without changing the transmit pulse, and without touching the regulator's allocation.

02The Approach

Pack symbols closer. Recover them smarter.

τ < 1 FTN MODE SYMBOLS COMPRESSED

FTN signaling transmits symbols faster than the Nyquist criterion permits, deliberately introducing controlled inter-symbol interference. The transmitter is unchanged in form — only the symbol period shrinks by a factor τ. The complexity moves to the receiver, where modern detection algorithms recover the data with negligible loss. The reward: higher throughput in the same bandwidth, and improved spectral efficiency without changing the modulation, the bandwidth, or the regulatory mask.

  • No additional spectrum required
  • Compatible with existing pulse shapes
  • Adaptable to current air interfaces
  • Scalable from baseband to mmWave
